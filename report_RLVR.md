# Research Report on RLVR (Robust Light‑Weight Visual Recognition)

## 1. Introduction

RLVR refers to **Robust Light‑Weight Visual Recognition**, a sub‑field of computer vision that focuses on developing efficient, low‑resource models for real‑time image and video recognition tasks. The core challenge is to balance model performance with computational and memory constraints, enabling deployment on edge devices such as smartphones, drones, and IoT cameras.

## 2. Scope and Motivation

- **Edge Deployment**: Demand for on‑device inference in autonomous vehicles, robotics, and AR/VR.
- **Energy Efficiency**: Power‑constrained environments require models with low FLOPs and memory footprints.
- **Robustness**: Models must maintain accuracy under varying lighting, occlusion, and domain shift.

## 6. Emerging Research Questions

1. **Neural Architecture Search (NAS) for Edge** – How to automate the design of ultra‑compact yet robust models? 
2. **Quantization & Pruning Trade‑offs** – Optimal strategies for maintaining accuracy while reducing model size. 
3. **Domain Adaptation under Resource Constraints** – Techniques to adapt models to new environments without full retraining. 
4. **Hardware‑Software Co‑Design** – Collaboration between ASIC designers and ML researchers for custom accelerators. 
5. **Explainability on Edge** – Lightweight methods for interpreting model decisions in real‑time.
